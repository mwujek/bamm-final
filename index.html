Music Licensing

The Problem: Licensing recorded music is extremely complicated, expensive and doesn’t scale. Multiple licenses are often required, and the rights may be held by a complex mixture of labels, publishers, and individuals.

Our Solution: BAMM.tv doesn’t license studio recordings; instead, we curate and produce live performances of songs at no cost to artists in exchange for a global, perpetual license. All of our content (including documentaries, podcasts, and music culture programming) is produced under the same license, making it simple for us to grant you a license to all or customized portions of the library.

Technology

The Problem: Getting access to great content is only the first step. In order to give your customers the quality user experience they expect, you need to deliver that content seamlessly to mobile, web and TV devices without interruption.

Our Solution: BAMM.tv has developed a robust backend system for managing and distributing content to any device, anywhere in the world. Our API consists of a powerful set of tools for creating custom apps or integrating features into your existing stack. And we utilize industry leading content delivery networks for the most reliable content delivery regardless of territory. Think of BAMM.tv as your music television studio in the cloud.

Differentiation

The Problem: Music content is available for free all over the Internet. By offering a branded music experience to your customers, you are competing with free. So how can you leverage music to keep customers in your online ecosystem?

Our Solution: Foster music discovery through exclusive, high-quality content. One frustration that comes with every song being available with one click is separating the great new music from the cumulative digital noise and the standard canon of popular music. Your customers don’t want access to all music - they want to discover good music, and they want that music to sound and look good, too. BAMM.tv is a curator of breaking artists, and we make content your customers will want to introduce to their friends. We are the taste-makers for the taste- makers.

Documentary

This One’s for You captures an artistic discourse between songwriters and their inspirations. Songwriters first give a musical performance for an audience of one – an individual who’s inspired the song in some way. A conversation follows, with both sides sharing reactions and personal experience with either the song or the topics the song addresses.

Many of our favorite songs are conversations in which we only hear one side of the story; This One’s for You captures what happens when the muse is given the microphone.

Animated Shorts

Musical Yearbook is a storytelling series that explores the moment when a piece of music becomes inseparable from we are. Music is the dog-ear. It preserves who we were and contextualizes who we've become. People from all walks–both music creators and music lovers, writers of novels and writers of software, and everyone in between—tell their stories, thereby collectively creating a yearbook where we can compare and contrast who we were at the same point in our lives.

To Go Shows

In an effort to produce performances that stand out from the endless stream of music videos posted online, BAMM.tv takes artists out of their traditional setting. To Go Show videos most often involve one continuous shot of the performer, and look to highlight a live performance taking place in an unusual, striking setting. This approach has been made popular by Vincent Moon of La Blogotheque, as well as other production companies. Artists are keen to really push these videos, too, because the performance is typically very different from the album version of the track.

Proving Grounds: Jambox LiveAudio Series

Client: Jambox

Project: BAMM.tv worked with product managers of LiveAudio, which is a key component to the fleet of Jambox speakers. BAMM.tv successfully pitched and produced Proving Grounds, an exclusive series introducing indie artists to a much wider audience. The program also educated the public about binaural recording practices. Content shot was then used in promotional materials throughout Jambox marketing campaigns. Jawbone also licensed one of the songs to use in marketing and promotional campaigns Lastly, our team produced an "About LiveAudio" short for the company. This content proved to be a versatile music marketing asset across multiple Jambox platforms (web, mobile, newsletters, and more).

Audyssey

Client: Audyssey

Project; BAMM.tv teamed up with Audyssey and San Francisco music staple Noise Pop (Treasure Island Music Festival, Noise Pop) to put on a series of pop-up concerts througout San Francisco. BAMM.tv was responsible for curating the artists, location, set-design, production, and post-production. Audyssey then incorporated the content into a larger, multi-city series of pop-up shows called The Neighborhood.tv Sessions, coordinating with similar events in L.A. and New York. The San Francisco events proved to be an effective, grassroots marketing campaign that built brand loyalty by connecting with the customer on a .more personal, intimate, and indie scale.
